## 2026 Targeting Analysis

The headline number is deceptive. Dem vote share fell across both counties in 2024 — but turnout surged district-wide, meaning Ehasz's raw vote totals likely held steady or grew while Fitzpatrick dramatically overperformed his ticket. This is the Fitzpatrick problem in a nutshell: he is not a generic Republican. He wins on candidate quality, not party loyalty. Peeling off his personal vote requires either a stronger top-of-ticket environment, a better-defined Democratic challenger, or both.

That said, the precinct data points to two distinct 2026 target universes.

**Base mobilization targets** are precincts already posting 50%+ Dem share but showing soft turnout relative to their registration advantage — primarily in the Montgomery County portion of the district. These voters are already persuaded; they just need showing up. GOTV investment here has a high floor and low risk.

**Persuasion/low-turnout targets** are precincts where Dem share sits in the 42–48% range and turnout dropped from 2022 to 2024 — likely irregular Dem voters who showed up for a governor's race but skipped the House contest. Montgomery 1981's +7.12-point swing suggests this universe is movable; something changed there between cycles, and understanding *why* — new housing, demographic shift, candidate contrast — should drive field deployment decisions in 2026.

The strategic priority is not running up the score in already-blue precincts. It's identifying the 15–20 persuasion precincts where a 3-to-5-point shift closes the margin, and treating them as the entire race.
