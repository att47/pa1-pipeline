## 2026 Targeting Analysis

The headline number is deceptive. Dem vote share fell across both counties in 2024. However, turnout surged district-wide, meaning Ehasz's raw vote totals likely held steady or grew while Fitzpatrick dramatically overperformed on the national ticket. This is the Fitzpatrick case in a nutshell: he is not a generic Republican. He wins on candidate quality, not party loyalty. Peeling off his personal vote requires either A) a stronger top-of-ticket environment, B) a better-defined Democratic challenger, or C) both.

The precinct data points to two distinct 2026 targets.

**Base mobilization targets** are precincts that already post 50%+ Dem share but show soft turnout relative to their registration advantage, primarily in the Montgomery County portion. These voters need to show up. GOTV investment here has a comparatively high floor and low risk.

**Persuasion/low-turnout targets** are precincts where the Dem share is 42–48% and turnout dropped from 2022 to 2024. Montgomery 1981's +7.12-point swing suggests this universe is movable; something changed there between cycles, and understanding *why* should drive field deployment decisions in 2026.

The strategic priority should be identifying the 15–20 persuasion precincts where a 3-to-5-point shift closes the margin, and treat them as the major focus of the race.
